# Discovery research findings — 41 target wallets

Generated by `scripts/profile-wallets.ts` over the 41 active wallets that
survived recency triage (of 70 leaderboard candidates). 299 entry snapshots
reconstructed from public chain data. Backtest by `scripts/backtest-discovery.ts`
replaying the production `scoreDiscovery`. Raw evidence: `wallet-profile.json`.
Shipped scanner thresholds: `discovery-profile.json`.

## Headline

The cluster is real and profitable, and its edge is **publicly explainable and
located exactly where the scanner looks**. Net realized PnL across the sample
is **+1497 SOL**; the median wallet runs a **37% win rate** with a **~54-second
median hold** and **~51% one-buy/one-sell** — precisely the fast, single-shot
sniper behavior the brief described. 74% of their entries carried a public
pre-entry signal, so a public-data scanner can find most of them.

## The four research questions

**1. What do the tokens they buy have in common?**
Measured at entry (medians): market cap **$7.5k** (p25 $3.6k, p75 $23k), age
**86 s**, **14 holders**, **~111 buyers/min**, dev co-deposit **~1.0 SOL**,
curve liquidity ~10 SOL. This matches the brief's "under $5–10k, seconds old,
one-buy/one-sell" almost exactly. Launch platform is **pump.fun** (191/211
launches); they trade the bonding curve and, after graduation, PumpSwap.

**2. What changed when performance improved?**
No single regime break across the cohort — 16/41 wallets improved in their
second half vs first, the rest were flat or worse, i.e. survivorship, not a
learned-edge inflection. The durable structural difference is **platform, not
time**: bonding-curve entries are net **+19.7 SOL (59% win)** while
graduated/PumpSwap entries are net **−11.8 SOL (44% win)**. Their money is made
pre-graduation; the post-graduation trades bleed it back. The scanner's
deliberate focus on the bonding-curve phase is therefore aligned with where the
edge actually is — this is the single most important validation in the dataset.

**3. Can the buy decisions be explained with public on-chain data? — Yes, 74%.**
74% of entries had at least one public pre-entry signal (a holder-velocity
surge, a recurring deployer/funder, or another target wallet already in). The
dominant, repeatable signal is **co-buying**: 205/299 snapshots had another
target wallet buying the same token, and **286 tokens saw ≥2 targets enter in
the same slot**. The remaining ~26% are same-block cluster buys on no prior
public activity — those are only detectable by catching the cluster *acting*
(the live smart-wallet feature), which is exactly how the scanner treats them.

**4. Recurring deployers, funders, patterns?**
- **Co-buys:** 675 tokens bought by ≥2 targets; 286 with same-slot entries.
- **Recurring deployers:** weak — only 3 deployers launched ≥2 of their bought
  tokens. They do **not** funnel through a few launchers.
- **Recurring funders:** moderate — 5 funding wallets recur, one across 7 tokens
  (`5tzFkiKscXHK5ZXCGbXZxdw7gTjjD1mBwuoFbhUvuAi9`).
- **Suspected cluster (the real find):** 25 non-target wallets appear early in
  ≥3 of the bought tokens — the top one in **20**. These are the cluster the
  targets run with; they are added to the scanner's smart-wallet set.
- **One-operator test:** inconclusive — all 41 targets are too high-volume for
  the funder walk to bottom out cheaply (0/41 resolved), so shared-funding
  among the targets could be neither confirmed nor ruled out.

## What the backtest changed in the profile

The auto-derived profile mis-scored the cluster in one clear way the "top
misses" exposed: it inherited the analyzer's **−10 fresh-disposable-creator
penalty**, but for this cluster a fresh creator wallet is the *norm*, not a rug
tell — the penalty was suppressing real winners (e.g. a +8.1 SOL entry scored
45 only because its creator had 4 prior txs). Neutralizing it
(`freshCreatorMaxTxs=0`) lifts PnL-weighted recall from 38% → 55% at the natural
threshold.

⚠️ **Read this neutralization as conditional, not settled.** The backtest only
contains the 299 tokens the cluster actually bought — all already quality-filtered
by smart wallets. Within that set, fresh-creator doesn't separate winners from
losers, but that is exactly the population where the penalty *shouldn't* fire.
Live, the scanner sees *every* pump.fun launch, including the rugs that fresh
throwaway creators churn out, where the penalty is genuinely protective. So the
profile turns it off because it was clipping the cluster's real picks, but this
trades away some rug protection. The alert-only paper run is what tells us
whether that trade is right — watch the precision (`discovery_alerts` peaks) of
fresh-creator alerts specifically before enabling autobuy.

The shipped profile also folds in the 25 cluster wallets (a *live* signal; the
historical snapshots can't see them, so backtest recall understates live
recall) and keeps the ~$60k pre-graduation mcap cap, which correctly excludes
the money-losing PumpSwap cohort. A separate code fix (commit "Parse the launch
window's oldest sigs…") ensures the scanner actually *catches* these same-slot
cluster entries live: it now parses the launch window's oldest signatures,
where same-slot snipers land, rather than the newest.

Shipped recall (replaying the production scorer):

| Alert score | recall | winner-recall | PnL-weighted |
|---|---|---|---|
| 40 | 64% | 64% | 76% |
| **50** | **50%** | **54%** | **70%** |
| 55 | 40% | 40% | 55% |
| 60 | 26% | 25% | 23% |

**Recommended operating point: `DISCOVERY_ALERT_SCORE=50`** (winner-recall 54%,
PnL-weighted 70%) — the biggest remaining miss scores 50, just under the
default 55. Keep `DISCOVERY_BUY_SCORE` higher (≥65) for the autobuy phase.

## Honest limits

- Recall is an **upper bound**: launch-slot bundling, mayhem state and mid-watch
  dev sells can't be reconstructed historically, so those vetoes never fire in
  the backtest (they will live, removing some entries).
- The 25 cluster wallets are **backtest-blind** (not recorded per snapshot), so
  true live recall should run *above* the table — the smart-wallet feature is
  the scanner's strongest, and most of its reach isn't counted here.
- Realized-only accounting: still-open bags are excluded, so per-wallet PnL is
  conservative.
- **`buyersPerMin` is measured differently live than in research.** Research
  counted distinct pre-entry buyers exactly; live, the scanner *estimates* it
  from a small sampled diversity ratio × exact tx velocity, so the floor (60)
  is noisier live than the table implies. `txPerMin` (from raw signature
  counts) is exact in both.
- Precision is **not** answered here (the wallets' picks are all positives);
  it comes from running the scanner alert-only and reading `discovery_alerts`
  outcomes — `npm run backtest-discovery -- … --db discovery.db`.
